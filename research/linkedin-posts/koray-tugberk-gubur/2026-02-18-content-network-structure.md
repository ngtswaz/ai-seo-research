Internal linking is not SEO housekeeping. It is your semantic content network made visible.

Every internal link is a signal to Google about which concepts are related and which pages are authoritative on those concepts.

Most B2B SaaS sites have a linking structure that looks like a random web. What they need is a hub-and-spoke model built around semantic relationships.

How to audit yours:

1. List your 10 most important topics
2. For each topic, identify your "hub" page (the most comprehensive treatment)
3. Check how many content pages link to that hub — should be 15-30 minimum
4. Check what anchor text those links use — should vary but include the core entity

Most sites I audit fail step 4. They use "click here" or post titles instead of semantic anchors.

AI can help you rebuild this at scale. I've used it to rewrite 800 internal links in a week.
