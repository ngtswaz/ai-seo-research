I've spent 8 months building AI agents for semantic SEO. Here's what I've learned.

The agents that work handle one specific task very well.
The agents that fail try to do everything.

Agents I've built that actually move the needle:

**Entity extraction agent:** reads a URL, returns all entities, their relationships, and which ones are missing vs competitors. Saves 4 hours per content audit.

**Content gap agent:** given a topic and a domain, identifies which subtopics competitors cover that you don't. Input for editorial calendar.

**Semantic update agent:** takes an existing article, identifies the outdated entities (companies that pivoted, features that changed, stats that aged), flags them for human review.

None of these write content. They make the brief smarter.

That's the right use of AI agents in SEO — not to replace the writer, but to replace the 4-hour research session before writing.
