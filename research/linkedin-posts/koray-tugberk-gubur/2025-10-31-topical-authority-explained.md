Topical Authority is not publishing a lot of content about one topic.

That's the most common misconception I see.

Topical Authority is about building a semantic content network where every entity, concept, and relationship in your niche is covered, connected, and authoritative.

What that actually means:

A SaaS company wanting topical authority on "CRM software" doesn't just publish "what is CRM." They map every entity in the CRM space:
- Features (contact management, pipeline, automation)
- Use cases (sales, support, marketing)
- Comparisons (vs spreadsheets, vs alternative tools)
- Integrations (every tool it connects to)
- Job roles (what sales reps need vs managers vs ops)

Then they cover every node in that map. Systematically.

AI makes this possible at a speed and scale that wasn't realistic 3 years ago.
