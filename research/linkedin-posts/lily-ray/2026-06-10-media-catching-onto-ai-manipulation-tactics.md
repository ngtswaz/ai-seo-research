The media is finally catching onto the popular SEO/GEO tactics that companies have been using to manipulate AI answers.

I was interviewed for this new article by Will Oremus of The Atlantic, which discusses self-promotional listicles, Reddit astroturfing and other common ways to influence AI responses.

The author even included a mention of my AI Overview joke experiment from last year, which ranked various SEOs as the best at silly things. Just checked and the experiment still works, over a year later 🤦🏽‍♀️

I know Google and OpenAI are trying to fix things - I keep tabs on this stuff daily and the results are gradually getting better over time. But if I'm being honest, I'm a bit surprised by how long it's taking them to do a better job cracking down on this wave of spam. Especially given how "front and center" these answers are for the user.
