GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is not SEO with a new name.

They share some foundations but the optimization targets are completely different.

SEO: optimize for crawlers indexing your page
GEO: optimize for LLMs synthesizing your content into answers

What works for GEO that doesn't move the needle in traditional SEO:

→ Structured, citable claims ("According to X study...")
→ Clear attribution and sourcing
→ Definitions that LLMs can lift verbatim
→ FAQ-style content that answers follow-up questions
→ Named methodology (something the model can reference by name)

B2B SaaS brands that invest in GEO now will own the citation layer before their competitors figure out the game has changed.

Most are still playing the old game.
