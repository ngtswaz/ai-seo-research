Just wrapped 50 episodes of the Optimize podcast.

The single most common theme across every guest who's figured out AI content: they treat it as a research tool first, a writing tool second.

The guests who've burned themselves with AI content almost always made the same mistake: they used AI to generate drafts from scratch, published too fast, and paid for it in Google's Helpful Content updates.

The guests doing it right:
1. Use AI to research the competitive landscape (what's already ranking, what's missing)
2. Write a detailed brief based on those gaps
3. Use AI to draft against that brief
4. Human rewrites the sections that require genuine experience
5. Publish, monitor, update

Step 4 is where most teams cut corners. It's also where all the E-E-A-T value comes from.
