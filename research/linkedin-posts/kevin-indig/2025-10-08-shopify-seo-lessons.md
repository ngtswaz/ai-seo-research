I ran SEO at Shopify for 3 years. Here's the one thing that mattered most at scale:

Content systems, not content pieces.

When you're trying to rank thousands of pages, the editorial process that works for 10 articles breaks completely. You can't hand-craft everything.

What we built instead:
1. Templates with locked components (what AI fills) and unlocked components (what humans write)
2. Quality gates at the brief stage, not the publish stage
3. Automated monitoring that flags content decay before it costs rankings

The brands doing AI content well in 2025 figured this out. They're not asking "should we use AI?" They're asking "which parts of the workflow should AI own vs humans?"

That question is worth 10x more than any prompt engineering trick.
