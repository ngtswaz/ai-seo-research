I've now seen 12 companies hit by Google's Helpful Content system in 2025.

Every single one had the same root cause: they used AI to scale content without solving quality.

The pattern:
1. Start publishing AI content (traffic holds or grows slightly)
2. Double down (traffic continues, confidence builds)
3. Hit a threshold — site-wide demotion
4. Scramble to fix it (average recovery time: 8-14 months)

The companies that avoided this weren't using less AI. They were using it differently.

They treated every AI-produced piece as a draft that needed genuine human expertise added before publishing. Not light editing — real contribution.

The ones that got hit treated AI as a publishing machine, not a drafting tool.

The difference between a draft and a publish-ready piece is exactly where most shortcuts get taken.
