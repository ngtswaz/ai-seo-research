Keywords are not the unit of SEO anymore. Topics are.

I've been saying this for 5 years. In 2025 it's finally undeniable.

Google's understanding of language has gotten sophisticated enough that stuffing a keyword 12 times tells it almost nothing. What tells it everything is whether your content covers the concept completely.

What that means practically:

Old way: target "content marketing tools" → write 2000 words with that phrase repeated
New way: target the concept of content marketing tooling → cover every related entity, use case, comparison, and user question

The difference in ranking outcomes between these two approaches is not small. It's the difference between page 3 and position 1.

This is also why AI content, when used naively, underperforms. It generates text around a keyword. It doesn't think in concepts.
