We switched our AI writing workflow from GPT-4 to Claude 8 months ago. Here's why and what changed.

The main difference in practice: Claude is less likely to confidently state wrong things.

For content that has to be accurate (which, at Ahrefs, is all of it), hallucination rate matters more than output quality. A beautifully written paragraph with a wrong statistic is worse than an awkward paragraph that's correct.

Our current workflow:
1. Brief written by human (non-negotiable)
2. Claude drafts section by section, not the full article at once
3. Each section reviewed for accuracy before moving to next
4. Human rewrites introduction and conclusion
5. Editor pass for voice consistency
6. Fact-check against primary sources

Time per article: ~3 hours (down from ~8 hours)
Accuracy rate: comparable to fully human-written
Output per writer per week: 3x what it was 18 months ago
