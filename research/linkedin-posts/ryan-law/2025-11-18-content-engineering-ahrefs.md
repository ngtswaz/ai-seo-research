We've been using AI to write content at Ahrefs for 18 months. Here's what I've learned.

The term "AI content" is useless. It doesn't describe what actually matters — which is what a human contributes and when.

We now call what we do "content engineering." It's more accurate.

The engineer's role:
- Define the brief (what the piece needs to accomplish, who reads it, what they need to walk away knowing)
- Curate the sources (what real-world data and examples should appear)
- Review for accuracy (AI confidently says wrong things)
- Write the parts that require judgment (conclusions, recommendations, anything with stakes)

The AI's role:
- Draft the structure
- Fill in established facts
- Generate alternatives when we're stuck
- Accelerate first drafts from 0 to 60%

The moment we named it "engineering" instead of "writing with AI," the quality went up. Framing matters.
