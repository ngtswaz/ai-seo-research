## "Is AI Cutting Into Your SEO Conversions?" (2025)
**URL:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/is-ai-cutting-into-your-seo-conversions

### Key findings

Kevin analysed organic traffic and conversion data from multiple B2B SaaS companies against the rollout of AI Overviews. Core findings:

- **Informational queries:** CTR dropped 18–32% where AI Overviews appeared
- **Navigational queries:** Largely unaffected — brand searches still drive clicks
- **Commercial/transactional queries:** Mixed — some saw increased CTR (users who clicked were more qualified), some saw volume drops

The most important distinction Kevin makes: **traffic loss ≠ revenue loss**. Companies that lost 20% of organic traffic to AIO sometimes saw conversion rates go up because the remaining traffic was more purchase-intent-focused.

### Implication for content strategy
Don't optimise purely for traffic. Optimise for the queries that drive pipeline. AI Overviews are filtering out low-intent users — for B2B SaaS, that might be net positive.

## "How to Think About AI Content at Scale" (2025)
**URL:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/ai-content-scale

### Key framework: The Content Yield Formula

Kevin introduces the concept of "content yield" — the ratio of pipeline generated to content produced.

> Content Yield = Pipeline Attributed to Content / Total Content Investment

Teams using AI naively increase the denominator (more content, same investment) without increasing the numerator (same pipeline or less). The result: lower yield.

Teams using AI strategically:
1. Identify the 20% of topics driving 80% of pipeline
2. Use AI to make those pieces 10x more comprehensive
3. Use AI to refresh decaying content faster
4. Measure yield quarterly, not traffic monthly

### What to do with this
Before any AI content initiative, establish a pipeline attribution model. Without it, you can't measure whether AI is helping or hurting.

## "The SEO Experiment Framework" (2025)
**URL:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/seo-experiment-framework

Kevin's approach to treating SEO like a product team treats feature development:

1. **Hypothesis** — "If we add comparison tables to informational content, CTR will increase"
2. **Test** — Apply to a controlled set of 20 pages, leave 20 unchanged
3. **Measure** — Compare CTR, rankings, and time-on-page at 60 days
4. **Decision** — Roll out, iterate, or reject
5. **Document** — Every experiment added to a shared "SEO learnings" doc

Results from 14 experiments Kevin ran in 2025 (full data in his LinkedIn posts):
- Content refreshes: +40% avg traffic
- Comparison tables: +22% CTR
- Thin page removal: +15% site-wide crawl efficiency

**AI application:** AI makes running more experiments cheaper — you can test 5 variations of a page in the time it used to take to write 1.
