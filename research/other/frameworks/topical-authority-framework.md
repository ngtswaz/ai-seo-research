## What it is

Topical Authority is a framework for structuring a website's content so that search engines (and LLMs) recognise it as the definitive source on a given topic. Coined and operationalised by Koray Tugberk GUBUR, it goes beyond keyword targeting to model the *semantic relationships* between concepts.

The formula Koray uses:

> **Topical Authority = Topical Coverage × Historical Data × Contextual Relevance**

---

## Core components

### 1. Topical Coverage
Every entity, sub-topic, related concept, and question inside a niche must be covered. Gaps in coverage = gaps in authority.

Example for a CRM SaaS:
- **Hub pages:** What is CRM, CRM for Sales Teams, CRM for Support Teams
- **Spoke pages:** Contact management, pipeline tracking, email automation, CRM integrations, CRM vs spreadsheets
- **Micro-topics:** How to import contacts into a CRM, CRM data migration checklist

The goal is a *semantic content network* — every node connected, every relationship documented.

### 2. Historical Data
Google rewards consistent publishing history on a topic. A site that has covered CRM for 3 years outranks one that published 200 articles last month, even with identical content quality.

Implication for AI content: bulk-publishing with AI doesn't shortcut this. The historical signal accrues over time.

### 3. Contextual Relevance
Pages must signal their relationship to each other through:
- Internal link anchor text (not "click here" — use semantic anchors)
- Consistent entity usage across pages
- Structured data / schema where applicable

---

## Why this matters for AI content production

AI makes it cheap to *identify* topical gaps (entity extraction, competitor analysis) and to *draft* spoke pages at scale. The bottleneck shifts to:
1. Mapping the semantic content network correctly before publishing
2. Maintaining publishing consistency (not one-time content dumps)
3. Ensuring internal linking reflects the semantic architecture

Koray has built AI agents that automate steps 1 and 3 — entity extraction agents and internal link rewrite agents. See: `research/linkedin-posts/koray-tugberk-gubur/2025-12-22-ai-agents-semantic-seo.md`

---

## How B2B SaaS teams apply it

1. Choose 3–5 core topic pillars (e.g. "CRM automation", "sales pipeline management", "CRM integrations")
2. For each pillar, map every entity, sub-entity, and question using a tool like Koray's Holistic SEO methodology or an AI extraction agent
3. Build hub pages for each pillar (comprehensive, authoritative)
4. Build spoke pages for every mapped entity/question
5. Connect all spokes to their hub with semantic internal links
6. Publish incrementally — don't dump 200 pages at once
7. Audit quarterly for new entities (new features, new competitors, new questions)
