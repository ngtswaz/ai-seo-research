# Product-Led SEO Framework
**Source:** Eli Schwartz — Author, *Product-Led SEO* | elischwartz.co
**URL:** https://elischwartz.co/book/
**Date compiled:** June 2026

---

## Core premise

Traditional SEO asks: *"What keywords should we target?"*
Product-Led SEO asks: *"Why would a user search for and find us?"*

The distinction sounds subtle. The implications are significant. Keyword-first SEO optimises for what people are already searching. Product-led SEO builds things that become what people search for.

---

## The framework

### Step 1: Start with user intent, not keywords
Before any content decision, ask:
- What job is a user trying to accomplish?
- At what stage of that job would they need us?
- What would they type when they're at that stage?

This produces content briefs tied to real user value, not search volume.

### Step 2: Build indexable product surfaces
The highest-leverage SEO in B2B SaaS is often not content — it's product features that generate indexable pages at scale:

- **Free tools** with shareable result pages (e.g., a CRM health score calculator where each result has its own URL)
- **Public directories** (integrations, templates, use case libraries, customer stories)
- **User-generated content** on your domain (community forums, Q&A, published reports)
- **Data pages** derived from your product's unique dataset (e.g., benchmark reports by industry)

These compound over time without a content team. They earn backlinks naturally. They convert higher because the user is already in the product context.

### Step 3: Evaluate content through the product lens
Before producing any piece of content, ask:
- Does this connect a user to our product in a meaningful way?
- Could a competitor publish the exact same piece? (If yes, reconsider.)
- Does this serve a buyer or just a random searcher?

### Step 4: Scale what works, not everything
Eli's explicit warning on AI: AI makes it cheap to publish 50 articles/month. That's only valuable if the content strategy is sound. Publishing 50 weak pieces with AI = 50x the damage of publishing 5 weak pieces manually.

## When NOT to use AI content (Eli's criteria)

Based on Eli's consulting work across 50+ SaaS companies:

1. **When you have no proprietary angle** — if the content is purely synthesised from public information, it adds nothing
2. **When the topic requires trust signals** — security, compliance, financial content needs verifiable expertise, not AI drafts
3. **When you haven't solved distribution** — more content makes distribution problems worse, not better
4. **When you're in a highly competitive niche with established players** — AI content can't overcome a 5-year domain authority gap

---

## Relevance to AI content production

Product-Led SEO is the most important *counter-framework* for B2B SaaS content teams. It doesn't oppose AI — it clarifies when AI-generated content creates value vs. noise.

The teams using AI most effectively in 2025–2026 are applying product-led thinking first: they identify the 10–20 topics that actually drive pipeline, then use AI to make those pieces definitively comprehensive. They're not using AI to publish on every keyword.
