# AIM Framework — AI in Content Teams
**Source:** Ross Simmonds — Foundation Marketing
**URL:** https://foundationinc.co/lab/ai-content-marketing/
**Date compiled:** June 2026

---

## What it is

The AIM Framework is Ross Simmonds' model for integrating AI across a content team's full workflow — not just writing. AIM stands for:

- **A — Accelerate** (using AI to speed up existing tasks)
- **I — Integrate** (embedding AI into team workflows as a permanent layer)
- **M — Multiply** (using AI to distribute and repurpose at scale)

Most teams only use AI for **A** (acceleration / drafting). The leverage is in **I** and **M**.

---

## The three legs

### A — Accelerate
Using AI to do faster what you already do manually.

Examples:
- AI drafts first 60% of an article; human finishes
- AI generates 10 headline variations; human selects
- AI writes meta descriptions in bulk
- AI summarises competitor content for research

**Risk:** Teams stop here and conclude "AI saves some time." They miss the real opportunity.

### I — Integrate
Embedding AI as a structural part of the content workflow — not an add-on.

Examples:
- Every content brief runs through an AI gap-analysis step before writing starts
- AI monitors content decay weekly and flags underperforming pages automatically
- AI classifies inbound content requests by topic cluster before assigning to writers
- AI generates the internal linking map before any page is published

**What this requires:** Someone owns the AI systems (the "AI Content Engineer" role). Without ownership, integration degrades back to ad-hoc acceleration.

### M — Multiply
Using AI to turn one piece of research into many formats and many placements.

Ross's documented example (Foundation):
- 1 research report (40 hrs to produce)
- → 12 LinkedIn posts
- → 3 email newsletter segments
- → 8 Twitter/X threads
- → 2 YouTube scripts
- → 5 podcast talking points
- → 4 blog posts
- Repurposing time with AI: ~6 hours

**The moat:** Distribution is still the competitive advantage. AI just makes distribution accessible to teams that previously couldn't afford it.

---

## Why this matters for B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS content teams typically have small headcounts (2–5 people) producing for multiple channels. The Multiply leg is where AI creates disproportionate leverage — a team of 3 can achieve distribution that used to require 8–10.

Foundation's own restructure (from Ross's LinkedIn):
- Before AI: 9 people, ~30 pieces/month
- After AI (AIM-structured): 5 people, ~120 pieces/month
