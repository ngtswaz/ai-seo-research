# Podcast Summary: "Rethinking SEO in the Age of AI"
**Guest:** Eli Schwartz
**Host:** Lenny Rachitsky
**Show:** Lenny's Podcast
**URL:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/rethinking-seo-in-the-age-of-ai-eli-schwartz
**Date:** September 2024
**Duration:** ~90 minutes

---

## Why this episode matters

Lenny's Podcast reaches a product and growth audience, not an SEO audience. Eli Schwartz is one of the only SEO practitioners who can credibly argue the product case for and against SEO. This episode is the clearest public statement of Eli's product-led framework applied to the AI era.

---

## Key arguments

### "Most companies shouldn't do SEO at all"
Eli's most provocative claim: for early-stage startups and companies without strong product-market fit, investing in SEO is a distraction. The argument:

- SEO compounds slowly (12–18 months to see meaningful results)
- If you can't afford to wait 18 months, the investment destroys runway
- The companies that succeed with SEO are the ones who can be patient
- AI has made the timeline *longer*, not shorter, because the market is more competitive

For B2B SaaS specifically: don't start SEO until you have a repeatable sales motion and know which customer segments convert.

### "The keyword is the wrong starting point"
Eli argues that keyword research as the entry point to SEO strategy is backwards. The right starting point is:

1. Who is your ideal customer?
2. What are they trying to accomplish?
3. At what moment in their journey would they search?
4. What would they search for at that moment?

Keywords emerge from this process — they're outputs, not inputs.

### "AI makes bad SEO more expensive, not cheaper"
When AI makes it easy to publish 50 articles a month, the cost of a bad SEO strategy multiplies. Companies without a clear content strategy who adopt AI content tools are accelerating their own failure.

Eli's specific warning: "The worst thing you can do with AI content is publish things that don't serve your customer. You just do it faster and at scale."

### The LLM disruption point
Eli was early (Sep 2024) in predicting what has since happened:

- Informational queries migrating from Google to ChatGPT/Perplexity
- "How to" and "what is" content becoming less valuable over time
- Proprietary data and original research becoming the only defensible content moat

His prediction: by 2026, the only B2B SaaS content that drives meaningful organic revenue will be either (a) deeply proprietary or (b) product-embedded (indexable product surfaces).

---

## Relevance to the playbook

This episode provides the clearest "when not to" framework for AI SEO content production. Before a B2B SaaS company builds an AI content system, they should be able to answer Eli's questions:

1. Do we have product-market fit?
2. Do we have 18+ months of runway for SEO to compound?
3. Do we have a content angle that no competitor can replicate with AI?

If the answer to any of these is no, the AI content playbook should start with a different question.
